## MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING (MOU)

**This Memorandum of Understanding (“MOU”) is entered into on [Date], by and between:**

1.  **Tashfeen Ekram** (hereinafter referred to as the **"Technical Lead"**)
2.  **Jawaid Ekram** (hereinafter referred to as the **"Partner"**)
3.  **OBAT Health / Anwar** (hereinafter referred to as **"OBAT"**)

### 1. Purpose & Project Reference
The Parties intend to collaborate on a joint grant application to the **Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation** for the development of **"Philanthropy OS: An AI-Driven Ecosystem for Accelerated Giving."** This MOU governs the relationship described in the technical specifications and implementation roadmap found at:
* **Project Repository:** [github.com/tekram/ai-giving-project](https://github.com/tekram/ai-giving-project/tree/main)
* **Detailed RFP Response:** [RFP_response.md](https://github.com/tekram/ai-giving-project/blob/main/RFP_response.md)

**Note on Iteration:** The Parties acknowledge that the technical specifications, repository files, and project drafts referenced above are **preliminary and subject to change.** As the development process and grant requirements evolve, these documents may be updated to reflect refined technical architectures or strategic shifts.

### 2. Pre-Proposal Commitment & Costs
* **Initial Resource Commitment:** OBAT/Anwar agrees to commit **$1,000 USD** to cover immediate development costs (LLM APIs, development tools, and infrastructure) required for the prototype and RFP submission phase.
* **Contingency:** In the event the grant is not awarded, OBAT may choose to continue using the developed platform. In this scenario, **OBAT agrees to cover all ongoing infrastructure costs** (e.g., Google Cloud/Firebase hosting, Gemini API fees) required to maintain their use of the platform.

### 3. Roles and Responsibilities
* **OBAT Health (Anwar):** * Provides active, hands-on feedback on the "Impact Passport" and "Agentic Giving" features on a **weekly cadence**.
    * Commits to **integrating the solution** into the official OBAT website upon successful validation.
    * Responsible for **recruiting three (3) additional charities** to the platform within six (6) months of launch to validate the sector-scale model.
    * Acts as the primary applicant and fiscal sponsor for the grant.
* **Tashfeen Ekram & Jawaid Ekram (The Developers):** * Responsible for the technical architecture, development of the "Impact Passport," Agentic Engine, and all technical grant documentation.

### 4. Post-Grant Compensation & Disbursement Schedule
Subject to the successful awarding of the grant, the total Project Fee of **$50,000 USD** shall be distributed to the Technical Lead according to the following milestones:
1.  **Grant Award Initiation:** **$10,000** payable immediately upon receipt of the grant reward from the Foundation.
2.  **Iterative Development Milestone:** **$25,000** payable upon completion and deployment of the next major iteration/beta version of the Philanthropy OS platform.
3.  **Operational Support:** The remaining **$15,000** shall be distributed in three (3) monthly installments of **$5,000** each following the completion of the second milestone.

### 5. Intellectual Property (IP) Ownership
* **Developer Ownership:** **All Intellectual Property (IP), including but not limited to source code, algorithms, and the "Philanthropy OS" architecture, remains the sole property of the Developers (Tashfeen Ekram and Jawaid Ekram).**
* **Usage License:** In consideration of their role as the primary test site and fiscal sponsor, OBAT is granted a **perpetual, irrevocable, and royalty-free license** to use the platform for its charitable missions. 
* **Commercialization:** The Developers retain the exclusive right to commercialize, license, or open-source the underlying technology for other applications or organizations.

### 6. Confidentiality and Data Governance
* **Data Protection:** All donor data will be handled with industry-standard encryption and anonymization in compliance with GDPR and ethical AI standards.
* **Non-Disclosure:** Proprietary technical methods and internal OBAT data are "Confidential Information" and will not be shared without prior written consent.

### 7. Compliance
* **Global Access:** The Parties agree to adhere to the Gates Foundation’s "Global Access" policy, ensuring that the knowledge gained and the solution developed are managed to benefit the public good.
* **Nonprofit Integrity:** All financial arrangements are structured as fair-market value for professional services to ensure compliance with 501(c)(3) regulations and prevent private inurement.
